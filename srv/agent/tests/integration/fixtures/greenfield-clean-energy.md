## How Tax Equity Partnerships Work

A typical tax equity partnership creates a special purpose entity that owns the renewable energy project, with ownership split between the developer and an investment fund (comprised of GCEC and institutional investors).

This structure efficiently monetizes federal tax incentives while providing attractive returns to all parties.

## Project Economics

- Total Project Cost: $14MM
- Developer Equity: $4.78MM (34%)
- Investment Fund (GCEC + Investor): $4.02MM (29%)
- Project-Level Debt: $5.20MM (37%)

## Investment Fund Structure
- GCEC: 0.01% ownership
- Institutional Investor: 99.99% ownership
- Investment Fund receives 99% of all tax credits and income/losses
- Developer retains 1% ownership initially

## Key Terms & Timeline
- Solar ITC Credit: $4.02MM (30% of eligible costs), with Investment Fund receiving 99% in Year 1
- Preferred Return: 1-1.5% annually on invested capital
- Cash Distributions: Investment Fund receives 1% of project cash flow, Years 1-5
- Flip Year: Year 6 structure "flips" to Developer with put/call option for Investment Fund exit

This structure reduces the developer's absolute cash outlay while allowing them to leverage their balance sheet.

The Investment Fund contributes capital at a discount to the tax credit value and receives immediate tax benefits plus ongoing cash returns.

## Regulatory Landscape: A Big Beautiful Bill

Understanding the Tax Credit Structure and Timeline

The regulatory environment for renewable energy tax credits remains favorable, with specific timelines and requirements that create urgency for project development and investment.

Through July 4, 2026 1
Projects beginning construction receive full 30% ITC (Section 48E) or $0.03/kWh PTC (Section 45Y) with a 4-year placed-in-service window.

2 After July 4, 2026.

Projects must be placed in service by the end of 2027 to qualify for credits; a tighter timeline increases execution risk.

2026

Onwards 3.

Strict prohibition on "specified foreign entities" (SFE) or "foreign influenced entities" (FIE) claiming credits.

4 Post-2032 Phase-Out.

Credits decrease annually for geothermal, storage, hydro, and fuel cells, reaching 0% for projects starting after January 1, 2036.

Available Bonus Adders: Corporate Tax Benefits

The Inflation Reduction Act established several bonus credit categories that can significantly enhance project economics:

- Energy Community Adder: 10% bonus for projects in former coal regions or brownfield sites
- Domestic Content Adder: 10% bonus for projects using U.S.-manufactured components
- Low-Income Community Adder: 10-20% bonus for projects benefiting disadvantaged communities

A critical advantage for institutional investors: corporations can utilize renewable energy tax credits and losses against all income, not just income from the renewable energy project itself.

This allows banks and other corporations with substantial tax liabilities to efficiently monetize these benefits across their entire business operations.
