# Applications

Busibox apps are specialized tools built on the platform. They share your authentication, your document access, and the same AI infrastructure. You access them all from the AI Portal.

## What Busibox Apps Are

Apps are web applications that run on Busibox. They're not separate products — they're integrated into the platform. When you open an app, you're already logged in. The app knows who you are and what you can access.

## Accessing Apps

From the AI Portal dashboard, you'll see a grid of app cards. Click any card to open that app. The apps you see depend on your permissions; your admin controls which apps are available to you.

## Core Apps

### AI Portal

The AI Portal is the main dashboard and your entry point. From here you can:

- Manage documents (upload, search, view status)
- Access admin settings (if you're an admin)
- Launch other apps
- View your home page and app library

### Agent Manager

Agent Manager is for working with AI agents. You can:

- Chat with agents
- Create and configure agents (if you have admin access)
- Use the workflow builder to design multi-step processes
- Test agents in the chat simulator

Most users will use Agent Manager primarily for chatting with agents. Admins can also create and tune agents for the team.

## Example Apps

Beyond the core apps, your organization may have additional tools:

- **Status Report** — Track projects and get AI-powered status updates. The agent helps you summarize progress, blockers, and next steps.
- **Estimator** — Cost estimation with Monte Carlo simulation. Useful for bidding, planning, and scenario analysis.

Other apps may be added over time. Each app appears as a card on the AI Portal home page.

## Apps Inherit Your Permissions

When you use an app, it respects your Busibox permissions:

- **Authentication** — You're already signed in. No separate login.
- **Document access** — Apps can only see documents you're allowed to see. If an app searches documents, it uses your visibility settings.
- **Roles** — Your roles determine what you can do in each app. Some features may be admin-only.

## Your Admin Controls Access

Your administrator decides:

- Which apps are installed and available
- Who can access each app (by role or user)
- How apps are configured (e.g., which agents they use)

If you don't see an app you expect, or you get an access error, contact your administrator.
